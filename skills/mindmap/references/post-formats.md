# LinkedIn Post Formats

## Format Library

Each format below includes structure, best use, and a hook template. When generating post ideas, tag each idea with its recommended format.

### 1. Story Post
**Structure**: Hook → Context → Conflict → Resolution → Lesson
**Best for**: Founding story, mistakes, lessons, customer stories
**Hook template**: "In [year/time], I [did/experienced something unexpected]..."
**Length**: 150-300 words

### 2. Framework Post
**Structure**: Hook → Problem → Framework (3-5 steps) → Why it works
**Best for**: Mental models, decision-making, processes, tactical content
**Hook template**: "I use a [N]-step framework for [decision/process]. Here it is:"
**Length**: 100-200 words

### 3. Hot Take
**Structure**: Provocative statement → Why most people are wrong → Your evidence → Nuanced conclusion
**Best for**: Spiky POVs, myth-busting, industry commentary
**Hook template**: "[Common belief] is wrong. Here's why:"
**Length**: 100-200 words

### 4. Listicle
**Structure**: Hook → Numbered list (5-10 items) → Closing thought
**Best for**: Tactical tips, tool stacks, lessons learned, observations
**Hook template**: "[N] things I wish I knew about [topic] before [experience]:"
**Length**: 100-250 words

### 5. Behind-the-Scenes
**Structure**: Hook → "Here's what it actually looks like" → Real details → Takeaway
**Best for**: Authenticity, day-in-the-life, building in public, process transparency
**Hook template**: "Everyone sees [the polished version]. Here's what it actually looks like:"
**Length**: 100-200 words

### 6. Data Post
**Structure**: Surprising stat/number → Context → Analysis → Implication
**Best for**: Metrics transparency, benchmarks, market analysis, customer insights
**Hook template**: "We analyzed [X data points]. The #1 finding surprised us:"
**Length**: 100-200 words

### 7. Vulnerable/Real Post
**Structure**: Honest admission → What happened → How you dealt with it → What you learned
**Best for**: Burnout, mistakes, imposter syndrome, failures, hard decisions
**Hook template**: "I need to be honest about something:"
**Length**: 150-250 words

### 8. "What I Learned" Post
**Structure**: Time marker → Situation → Lessons (3-5) → Current application
**Best for**: Retrospectives, career lessons, customer lessons, book takeaways
**Hook template**: "[Time period] of [doing X] taught me [N] things:"
**Length**: 100-250 words

### 9. Question/Poll Post
**Structure**: Context/observation → Binary or multi-option question → Your take (optional)
**Best for**: Engagement, community building, market research, starting debate
**Hook template**: "Quick question for [audience]:"
**Length**: 50-100 words

### 10. Comparison Post
**Structure**: Thing A vs Thing B → Why the comparison matters → Your verdict with reasoning
**Best for**: Bootstrap vs VC, build vs buy, speed vs quality, old way vs new way
**Hook template**: "[Option A] vs [Option B]. After [experience], here's what I've learned:"
**Length**: 100-200 words

### 11. Prediction Post
**Structure**: Bold prediction → Evidence/reasoning → Timeline → What to do about it
**Best for**: Industry commentary, trends, market analysis, vision posts
**Hook template**: "In [timeframe], [bold prediction about industry]. Here's why I believe this:"
**Length**: 100-200 words

### 12. "Advice I'd Give" Post
**Structure**: Audience specification → Advice items → Why (from personal experience)
**Best for**: Career lessons, founder advice, hiring advice, product advice
**Hook template**: "If I were [starting over / advising a new founder / hiring today], here's what I'd say:"
**Length**: 100-250 words

---

## Format Selection Guide

Match format to content type:

| Content Type | Best Formats |
|-------------|-------------|
| Personal story | Story, Vulnerable/Real |
| Industry opinion | Hot Take, Prediction |
| Teaching/expertise | Framework, Listicle, "What I Learned" |
| Metrics/data | Data Post, Behind-the-Scenes |
| Engagement builder | Question/Poll, Hot Take |
| Thought leadership | Prediction, Framework, Comparison |
| Recruiting content | Behind-the-Scenes, Story |

## Hook Principles

Strong hooks share these traits:
- **Specific**: Use numbers, names, timeframes — not vague generalizations
- **Tension**: Create curiosity gap or mild controversy
- **Personal**: Signal lived experience, not generic advice
- **Short**: First line should be under 15 words
- **Pattern-breaking**: Avoid "I'm excited to announce..." and other LinkedIn cliches
